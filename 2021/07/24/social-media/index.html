From social media to social impact.

link: https://www.ted.com/talks/amonge_sinxoto_from_social_media_to_social_impact_jan_2019

The presenter emphasized that social media makes everything accessible and gives support to make everyone’s voice heard. She came up with four key points to support her idea, which is investing in a cause, finding people who have passion, preparing and populating a remedy, and be consistent with one’s idea.

Obviously, social media provides a platform for active mind, allowing them to spread, and be known to more people. The presenter gave us the example of the invitation of giving speech for several famous university, or even chatting with president, after she decided to stop waiting for someone else to speak on behalf on African teenagers. However, that is the positive side of the social media. There are still presence of unhealthy websites, and extremist publish reactionary views on the Internet. The negative impact the social media brings to the society can not be ignored.

Why you should quit social media?

link:https://www.ted.com/talks/cal_newport_why_you_should_quit_social_media?fbclid=IwAR3t_FIS0_FsAhiPAeT0hVijOgg0YFOIeR76wuzUCrePAvT-lkixxXWIP6o

The presenter called on audiences to quit social media. His brief idea was that he still had friend, knew what was going on in the world and collaborated with people all around the world. People would be happier and have more sustainability.

I partly agree with the idea that social media is one kind of entertainment, and is designed to be addictive. Moreover, they may have negative impact such as permanently reducing one’s capacity for concentration. However, the presenter denied the potential opportunities provided by social media, which seemed to be ridiculous because it is quite obvious that social media provides job opportunities and brings benefits for self-media, and their followers, who is described as people who can be regardless by the presenter, are the major source of income for this industry

Social media&Youth–an intricate relationship

link:https://vimeo.com/268987063

It is always said that the Internet has a negative impact on people – trapping them in a virtual world and causing metal damage, like depression, anxiety and procrastination.

The presenter mentioned the cycle of damage, who said we always shared happiness, like family gathering and lively parties, which would make a lonely people feel much lonelier if he saw them on social media. However, as far as I am concerned, that’s not always the case. Firstly, there are still large amount of people who spread there negative emotion on the Internet, so what the presenter said doesn’t always happen. Moreover, for students who stay aboard on their own and feel lonely, chatting with friends or parents and sharing stories is a kind of spiritual comfort. This may be a motivation for them to move on. The opinion the presenter shares is for special cases only.

Could social media envy be good to you?

link:https://www.cbsnews.com/video/social-media-envy-could-it-be-good-for-you/

There are two kinds of envy exist. Benign envy is good envy which provides motivation, while malicious envy is a feeling of undercutting others so that they look better. The social media envy being talked about is benign envy. People most like us and the people we connect to are the people most like us, so we feel it’s attainable in order for us to feel envy.

We were taught to chase others and go beyond them if it is possible, and I believe most of us grow up with this kind of mentality. Neijuan (involution) is a specific name for this phenomenon. When being compare to others, people are always guilty with themselves of their weakness. I have to say it is a inspiring atmosphere for young people who just graduated, entering the society to work, but it may put too much stress on the middle-aged.
