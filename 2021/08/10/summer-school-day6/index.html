summer school day6

Posted on 2021-08-10 | | 阅读数

Today is the sixth day of global summer school. We were told to watch videos about several experiments and identified transgenes of drosophila melanogaster. Five properties were given.

GFP - Expresses a protein cloned from a jellyfish, which is affected by ultraviolet light, in the fat body cells of the larvae.

lacZ – The lacZ gene from E.coli encodes beta-galactosidase, an enzyme that cleaves lactose into glucose and galactose.

Dam – Expresses the DNA adenine methyltransferase (Dam) protein from E.coli (when crossed to a GAL4 line) that allows researchers to study gene regulation and chromatin in Drosophila. The plasmid carrying this gene will amplify with primer pair1. It will give a 550 base pair product.

Dam-gene X– Expresses a protein fused to Dam(when crossed to a GAL4 line). This gene will amplify with primer pair 2. It will give a 400 base pair product.

Gene Y – Identified in a screen looking for thermosensitive behavioural phenotypes in drosophila. When expressed in neurons, it will exhibit a phenotype at warmer temperature(>=30 degree centigrade).

The first experiment is lacZ activity.

Firstly, retrieve a wandering L3 larva of each mystery genotype and place each one in a seperate 1.5ml tube. Wash once in cold in 700 ul of cold PBS, and then remove all the PBS and dispose of it in the waste pot.

Secondly, pipette 300 ul of 50 mm ONPG substrate solution into each tube. While transferring for each tube, without ejecting all liquid from the tip, repeatedly jab and crush the larva with the tip. Vortex mixer can be used to help.

Thirdly, eject the remaining liquid from the tip and mix well by pipetting up and down.

After a few minutes - appearance of yellow colouration for reaction mixture containing larva expressing lacZ.

The second experiment is LacZ Expression in Larvae.

A wandering L3 larva(identified in the first experiment) is retrieved and washed in cold PBS.

Of the given 0.2% X-gal straining solution, a droplet is pipetted onto a glass slide.

Larval tissues are dissected out using cold PBS(900ul) in a small petri dish (with a black Sylgard bottom) under a dissecting microscope.

The dissected tissues are transferred into the droplet of the X-gal staining solution on the glass slide.

Vaseline is applied to four points on the slide where the coverslip’s corners will be.

The coverslip is placed on the Vaseline, pushing down each corner individually until the coverslip is just in contact with the X-gal staining solution.

Staining room temperature is conducted over 20 mins.

As a result, a blue color is produced.

The third one is Fluorescence Analysis. A plate containing all strains of the larvae, a glass slide and some tweezers are taken to the fluorescence microscope.

On larvae from each strain is lined up on the slide before placing on the microscope to allow the different larvae to be compared side by side.

It is obvious to see which one is DFP positive.

The fourth experiment is thermosensitivity assay.

Vials containing adult flies are placed in a 37 degree centigrade water bath for 5 min.

For one vial, its contents are poured onto a dry ice-cooled platform to anaesthetise them.

The files are observed under the microscope to identify differences in the population.

Once any differences between the ones that were paralyzed and the ones that were not are identified, a brush is used to transfer one group into the original vial and the other into a new vial, thus separating them.

To check the hypothesis, all the flies are allowed to wake up and then both vials containing separated adult flies are placed into the 37 degree centigrade water bath, again for 5 min, nothing any dichotomy in the paralysis phenotype.

It is observed that one of the tube have flies that lie at the bottom, so the flies in that tube are separated according to the gender, and repeat the experiment. It turns out that only female flies are sensitive to the temperature while male are not.

The four experiments showed me the magic of changing variable during the process. It is the first time I fins this method efficient and time-saving.
