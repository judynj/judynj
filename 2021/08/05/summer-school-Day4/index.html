summer school Day4

Posted on 2021-08-05 | | 阅读数

Today is the first day of global summer school organized by Imperial College.

The first lecture is clinical skills. The professor first introduce ABCDE principle. A stands for airway, because airway obstruction is a medical emergency. Doctors should judge whether the patients can talk, are they unconscious, do they need airway protection, is there any possibility of spinal injury and whether they can remove obstruction. The slide below shows the way to protect airway.

B stands for breathing. Breathing could be compromised because of life threatening conditions, and doctors should look, listen and feel for the general sigh of respiratory distress. The count respiratory rate should be 12-18 per minute.

C stands for circulation. Doctors should look at the hands and digits and judge whether they are blue or not, and assess capillary refill time should be less tan two seconds. The count pulse rate should be in normal range, which is 60-90 beat per minute.

D stands for disability. The pupillary reflex, level of consciousness, temperature, blood sugar and drug chart of patients should be checked.

E stands for exposure. That means doctors should check whether there is rash due to allergy, Pedal Edema, or DVT(deep vein thrombosis).

Then the professor show us the definition of professional world related to blood pressure. It is the force of blood pushing through the arteries. Systolic blood pressure (SBP) is the maximum pressure in the aorta at the end of systole(when blood is ejected into the arteries during contraction). Diastolic blood pressure(DBP) is the minimum pressure in the aorta at the end of diastole(between heartbeats when the heat is relaxed). When blood pressure is elevated, this is “hypertension” and leads to many complications. Moreover, high blood pressure may lead to serious complication like heart attack, stroke and kidney failure, but they can be treated with life style modification and drug.

The professor then showed us several videos of checking the patients’ bodies. That reminds me of the dialogue between doctors and me when I went to hospital. I realize that the questions they asked me were more serious and professional than I thought after having the lecture.

The second lecture is investigative medicine. The professor firstly shows us some background of endocrinology.

And the difference between nervous system and endocrine system.

The structure of one specific part of brain and the organs that each of them control.

The professor them gave us five cases and asked us to enter the breakout room for discussion. The case was hard, but with the help of mentor and searching, we finally dealt with it successfully. I had never heard of some of the concepts and words in the case, and native English speaker in our academic group really helped a lot. That made me realize the importance of communication and cooperation when dealing with tasks.
